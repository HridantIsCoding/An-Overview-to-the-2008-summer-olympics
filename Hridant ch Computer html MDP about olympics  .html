An overview of the 2008 Summer Olympics by Group-1 of Class-7C

Introduction

The 2008 Summer Olympics, officially known as The Games of the XXIX Olympiad, were held in Beijing, China. The event took place from August 8th to August 24th, 2008, A total of 10,942 athletes from 204 National Olympic Committees competed in 28 sports and 302 events.The motto of the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing was "One World, One Dream"

History of the Beijing Olympics

The Beijing 2008 Olympics were significant for several reasons. It was the first time that China hosted the Olympics. The Games were auspiciously scheduled to begin at 8:08 pm on the eighth day of the eighth month of 2008 in Beijing. It was a huge milestone in China's history, marking their growing dominance on the global stage.

The Torch Relay

The Torch Relay had many events and unexpected issues. About the Torch Relay:

  • The Torch Relay had the theme "Journey of Harmony".
  • The Torch Relay began at 24th March 2008, in Olympia, Greece. From there, it traveled across Greece to Panathinaiko Stadium in Athens, and then to Beijing, arriving on 31st March.
  • This Torch Relay was the longest in the history of Olympics with a distance of 137,000 kms and lasted 130 days and 21,880 torchbearers carrying the event.
  • The Olympic Torch was based on traditional scrolls and used a traditional Chinese design known as the "Propitious Clouds". The torch was designed to remain lit in 65 km/h (40 mph) winds, and in rain of up to 50 mm (2 in) per hour.
  • The Event faced issues like Anti-China protests and the Sichuan Earthquake which hit Western China.

The Olympic Village

The 2008 Summer Olympics was held in 37 venues, including 11 pre-existing venues, 12 new venues, and eight temporary venues. The major ones are:

  • Beijing National stadium - Hosted the Opening and Closing Ceremonies, athletic events including long jump , and the football final.
  • Water cube - Also known as the National Aquatics Center located in the Olymic Green hosted all the water sports like Swimming and Diving.
  • Beijing National Indoor Stadium - One of the main venues in the Olymic Green, it hosted Artistic gymnastics, Trampolining and Handball.

Our Game, Long Jump

The sport Long Jump was hosted in the Beijing National Stadium. 32 nations and 38 athletes participated in the Men's long jump event. 33 nations and 42 athletes participated in the Women's long jump event.

Statistics

Men's Long Jump:

  • Gold - Irving Saladino of Panama won the gold medal with a jump of 8.34 meters.
  • Silver - Khotso Mokoena of South Africa won the silver medal with a jump of 8.24 meters.
  • Bronze - Ibrahim Camejo of Cuba won the bronze medal with a jump of 8.20 meters.

Women's Long Jump:

  • Gold - Maurren Higa Maggi of Brazil won the gold medal with a jump of 7.04 meters.
  • Silver - Blessing Okagbare of Nigeria won the silver medal with a jump of 6.91 meters.
  • Bronze - Chelsea Hammond of Jamaica won the bronze medal with a jump of 6.79 meters.

Major Events

Some of the most memorable events of the 2008 Olympics include:

  • Abhinav Bindra - Won India's first individual Olympic gold medal in the men's 10m air rifle shooting event.
  • Michael Phelps - The American swimmer won a record 8 gold medals in a single Olympics.
  • Usain Bolt - The Jamaican sprinter set world records in the 100m and 200m races, winning both events with ease.
  • Gymnastics - The Chinese gymnastics team won multiple gold medals, showcasing their dominance in the sport.
  • Opening Ceremony - The opening ceremony was a grand spectacle, featuring a huge performance with traditional Chinese culture and modern technology.

Conclusion

The Beijing 2008 Olympics were a major success, both in terms of athletic achievement and cultural impact. The games left a lasting legacy for China and the world, highlighting the power of sport to bring people together across cultures.
